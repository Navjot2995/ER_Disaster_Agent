# Quick Sepsis Recognition Protocol (qSOFA) & Systemic Inflammatory Response Syndrome (SIRS)

## Triage Warning Signs for Sepsis
A patient presenting with suspected infection MUST be screened for sepsis using the following criteria. 

If infection is suspected, evaluate for SIRS Criteria (2 or more = High Suspicion, URGENCY: RED or High YELLOW):
1. Temperature > 38.0°C (100.4°F) or < 36.0°C (96.8°F).
2. Heart Rate (HR) > 90 beats per minute.
3. Respiratory Rate (RR) > 20 breaths per minute OR PaCO2 < 32 mmHg.
4. White Blood Cell Count (WBC) > 12,000/mm3 or < 4,000/mm3, or > 10% bands (if labs available).

## qSOFA Criteria (Quick Sequential Organ Failure Assessment)
Used for rapid bedrock clinical prediction of poor outcome in patients with suspected infection.
Evaluate for the following (Score 2 or 3 -> URGENCY: RED, prepare for rapid escalation):
1. **Altered Mental Status** (GCS < 15 or sudden confusion).
2. **Fast Respiratory Rate** (>= 22 breaths per minute).
3. **Low Blood Pressure** (Systolic BP <= 100 mmHg).

**Action Items for Sepsis**:
- Initiate rapid IV fluid resuscitation (usually 30 mL/kg crystalloid).
- Administer broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour.
- Measure serum lactate.
- Draw blood cultures prior to antibiotics.
