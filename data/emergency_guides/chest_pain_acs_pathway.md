# Chest Pain and Suspected Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS) Pathway

## High-Risk Features (URGENCY: RED)
Patients presenting with chest pain should be immediately evaluated for the following:
- Pain radiating to the left arm, neck, or jaw.
- Diaphoresis (heavy sweating) accompanying chest pain.
- Hemodynamic instability (Systolic BP < 90 mmHg, HR > 120 bpm).
- Syncope or altered mental status.
- New onset of severe shortness of breath.

## Moderate-Risk Features (URGENCY: YELLOW)
- Chest pain that is atypical but patient has strong risk factors (diabetes, existing coronary artery disease, age > 65).
- Pain reproduced somewhat by palpation but still concerning.
- Normal ECG on past visits but ongoing chest discomfort.

## Low-Risk Features (URGENCY: GREEN)
- Sharp, fleeting pain that worsens significantly with deep inspiration or coughing (pleuritic) without shortness of breath or hypoxemia.
- Pain completely reproducible with light palpation (musculoskeletal).
- Young patient (< 35) with no risk factors and normal vitals.

**Clinical Mandate**: Any adult patient > 30 presenting with non-traumatic chest pain must receive an ECG within 10 minutes of arrival. DO NOT dismiss chest pain as GREEN without an ECG if the patient has any cardiac history.
