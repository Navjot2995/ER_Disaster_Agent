# WHO Basic Emergency and Trauma Care Guidelines

World Health Organization protocols for rapid trauma and acute life support in resource-limited settings.

## ABCDE Approach (Primary Survey)
All arriving critical patients must be assessed through the ABCDE sequence immediately.

### A - Airway
- Verify airway patency. Look for foreign bodies, swelling, or massive facial trauma.
- Action: If the airway is obstructed, open it using jaw thrust or head-tilt-chin-lift. Consider placement of an artificial airway.
- **URGENCY: RED** (Any airway compromise).

### B - Breathing
- Check respiratory rate and chest movement. Are breath sounds equal?
- Look for signs of tension pneumothorax or massive hemothorax.
- Action: Administer high-flow oxygen. Needle decompression for suspected tension pneumothorax.
- **URGENCY: RED** (Absent or asymmetrical breathing, severe hypoxia).

### C - Circulation
- Address external massive hemorrhage with direct pressure or tourniquets.
- Evaluate for hemorrhagic shock: cool extremities, tachycardia, delayed capillary refill (> 3 seconds), narrow pulse pressure.
- Action: Establish IV access (2 large bore IVs) and begin fluid resuscitation with crystalloid.
- **URGENCY: RED** (Active severe bleeding or signs of shock).

### D - Disability
- Rapid neurological assessment: AVPU scale (Alert, Voice, Pain, Unresponsive) or GCS.
- Action: Document baseline. Prevent secondary brain injury (maintain oxygenation and blood pressure). Check blood glucose.
- **URGENCY: RED** (P or U on AVPU scale, GCS <= 13).

### E - Exposure/Environmental Control
- Fully expose the patient to locate all hidden injuries while maintaining warmth to prevent hypothermia (the lethal triad: hypothermia, coagulopathy, acidosis).
- Remove wet clothing. Provide warm blankets.
