# Adult Trauma Triage Guidelines (Offline Protocol)

## Mechanism of Injury (MOI)
- Fall from > 20 feet (1 story = 10 ft) -> URGENCY: RED
- High-risk auto crash (intrusion > 12 inches, ejection from vehicle, death in same passenger compartment) -> URGENCY: RED
- Auto vs. pedestrian/bicyclist thrown, run over, or with > 20 mph impact -> URGENCY: RED
- Motorcycle crash > 20 mph -> URGENCY: RED

## Anatomy of Injury
- All penetrating injuries to head, neck, torso, and extremities proximal to elbow or knee -> URGENCY: RED
- Chest wall instability or deformity (e.g., flail chest) -> URGENCY: RED
- Two or more proximal long-bone fractures -> URGENCY: RED
- Crushed, degloved, mangled, or pulseless extremity -> URGENCY: RED
- Amputation proximal to wrist or ankle -> URGENCY: RED
- Pelvic fractures -> URGENCY: RED
- Open or depressed skull fracture -> URGENCY: RED
- Paralysis -> URGENCY: RED

## Physiologic Criteria
- Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) <= 13 -> URGENCY: RED
- Systolic Blood Pressure (SBP) < 90 mmHg -> URGENCY: RED
- Respiratory Rate < 10 or > 29 breaths per minute (< 20 in infant aged < 1 year) -> URGENCY: RED
